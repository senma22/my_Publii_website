DH 101 - Introduction to Digital Humanities

Course Description: 

This course is an introduction to the Digital Humanities, its methods, theories, and applications in humanistic research. It covers a variety of digital tools and approaches to organize, explore, understand, present and tell stories with data. In this course, you will learn how to reverse engineer DH projects to understand how they were built; identify, use, and discuss the advantages and disadvantages of different tools and methodologies; develop strong humanistic research questions that can be answered through digital research methods; conduct original research; and build a collaborative digital project. You will also learn how to organize and clean data, develop charts, create spatial and network visualizations, work with a content management system, and use basic text analysis tools to explore qualitative data. (Taken from course syllabus)

Final Project : COVIDFACTS - Website

Our final project focused on using Tableau and other Digital Huamnities tools to explore a COVID - 19 dataset. 

Data: 

The dataset is from “Our World in Data”. It contains data from over 207 countries, including cases, deaths, and continents, vaccinations, and testing, among others. Data was collected by many researchers who have been collecting COVID data from all around the world since the very beginning of the COVID outbreak, and it is being updated on a timely basis. 

Our research focused on two aspects: 

  • What factors result in high mortality rate during COVID-19 pandemic?
  • What is the correlation between the vaccination rate and GDP per capita?

Methods: 

  • Embedded 7 interactive data visualizations using Tableau to help audience explore the dataset 
  • Embedded a timelineJS to mark and trace important events throughout the pandemic
  • In-depth analysis on our findings and how they are related to our learnings in DH 101 course
